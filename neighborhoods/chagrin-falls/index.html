About Chagrin Falls

Tucked into the wooded hills of eastern Cuyahoga County, about 20 miles southeast of downtown Cleveland, Chagrin Falls is a postcard New England-style village built around the natural waterfall that gives it its name. The falls cascade right through the center of town, framed by historic brick storefronts, the iconic Popcorn Shop, art galleries, boutiques, and locally owned restaurants — making downtown one of the most walkable and charming in Northeast Ohio.

Beyond downtown, Chagrin Falls offers tree-lined streets, larger wooded lots, and a mix of historic and custom-built homes. The village is served by the highly regarded Chagrin Falls Exempted Village School District and is convenient to Kenston schools as well, and its small-town feel, strong sense of community, and easy access to Cleveland's east side suburbs make it one of the most sought-after addresses in the region.

Livability & Community
