About Brook Park

Located just southwest of Cleveland and bordering Cleveland Hopkins International Airport, Brook Park has long been a hub for aviation, aerospace, and manufacturing — anchored by NASA's Glenn Research Center, one of the agency's premier propulsion research facilities. The city's central location and easy access to I-480 and I-71 make it a practical, affordable option for commuters across Greater Cleveland.

Brook Park is also entering an exciting new chapter: it has been selected as the site of the Cleveland Browns' new $2.4 billion domed stadium, part of a larger 178-acre mixed-use development with hotels, restaurants, and retail slated to open in 2029. Combined with its affordable housing stock, strong industrial employment base, and proximity to the airport, Brook Park is poised for significant growth and renewed investment in the years ahead.
