About Lakewood

Just west of downtown Cleveland along the southern shore of Lake Erie, Lakewood is one of Northeast Ohio's most walkable and densely charming communities. Tree-lined streets are filled with classic Tudor, Colonial, and bungalow-style homes built in the early 20th century, many within easy walking distance of the city's bustling Detroit Avenue corridor — home to dozens of locally owned restaurants, coffee shops, breweries, boutiques, and live music venues.

Lakewood's real draw, though, is its connection to the water. Lakewood Park stretches along nearly a mile of Lake Erie shoreline and is home to the Solstice Steps, a terraced limestone amphitheater that has become one of the region's most beloved spots to watch the sunset. Add in strong city services, an engaged community, and an easy commute into downtown Cleveland, and it's easy to see why Lakewood consistently ranks among the most desirable suburbs in Cuyahoga County.

Livability & Community

Lakewood (ZIP 44107) earns an AARP Livability Index score of 61 out of 100 — above the national average — with especially strong marks for transportation, neighborhood character, and civic engagement. The area's job base is anchored by healthcare, professional services, and manufacturing employers.
